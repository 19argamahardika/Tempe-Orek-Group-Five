Tempe Orek

A Delicious Traditional Indonesian Dish

Tutorial Video
Flipbook

Description

Tempe orek is a traditional Indonesian dish, specifically from the land of Java, made from tempeh that is either diced or sliced into strips and cooked with distinctive spices, namely palm sugar and tamarind.

Tempe orek is generally divided into two variations: dry tempe orek and wet tempe orek. In dry tempe orek, the palm sugar is cooked until it reduces and caramelizes evenly, coating the tempeh pieces with a layer of palm sugar and spices, creating a dry and slightly hard texture. In contrast, in wet tempe orek, the palm sugar does not caramelize evenly, leaving some of the uncaramelized palm sugar as a sauce, giving the tempe orek a soft texture that is best enjoyed while hot.

Purpose

The purpose of making Tempe Orek is to create an easy, nutritious, and tasty meal that complements various traditional Indonesian dishes.

Ingredients

  • 2 block of tempeh
  • 5 shallots
  • 3 cloves of garlic
  • 3 curly red chilies
  • 2 block of palm sugar
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 1 tsp tamarind
  • Seasoning to taste
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare all the ingredients.
  2. Cut the tempeh into matchstick-sized pieces.
  3. Thinly slice the garlic, shallots, and curly red chili.
  4. Fry the tempeh until golden.
  5. Once the tempeh is cooked, transfer it to a bowl.
  6. Stir-fry the garlic, shallots, and chili, then transfer them to the bowl with the tempeh.
  7. In the same pan, add palm sugar, a little water, tamarind, salt, and seasoning. Stir until well combined.
  8. Once the palm sugar has melted and the mixture has thickened, add the tempeh and stir-fried spices.
  9. Turn off the heat and mix the palm sugar mixture, spices, and tempeh until well combined.
  10. Serve in a bowl to enjoy, and the delicious tempe orek is ready to be savored!
